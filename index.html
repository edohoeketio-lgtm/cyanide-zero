Nigeria produces over 14,000,000 tons of cassava peel waste each year. Left to rot, it releases methane and leaches cyanide into our groundwater. CYAN_0 maps the industrial shift.

The Toxic
Toll

01 / CYANIDE RUNOFF

Sterilizing The Soil

Decomposing peels release hydrocyanic acid, sterilizing the soil and poisoning local water tables around Garri processing hubs.

02 / METHANE THREAT

Greenhouse Emissions

Mountains of wet peels ferment in the open air, releasing methane—a greenhouse gas 28x more potent than CO2.

03 / HIDDEN POTENTIAL

High-Energy Biomass

Cassava peels are not garbage. They are carbohydrate-rich biomass waiting to be processed into industrial resources.

Start The Cycle

1/ GATHER

Collect from Hubs

Gather wet peels directly from local Garri processing hubs before fermentation begins.

2/ EXTRACT

Grate & Press

Grate the peels and press immediately to drain the toxic cyanide juice (hydrocyanic acid).

3/ NEUTRALIZE

Sealed Fermentation

Ferment the pressed mash in sealed bags to neutralize any remaining toxins safely.

4/ UPCYCLE

Sun-dry HQCP

Sun-dry the mash into High-Quality Cassava Peel (HQCP) ready for industrial use.

Beyond The Peel

HQCP is just the beginning. The resources extracted from cassava waste fuel multiple industries.

AGRICULTURE

HQCP Livestock Feed

Cheaper, healthier poultry and livestock feed replacing expensive maize.
ENERGY

Bio-Ethanol

Clean cooking fuel replacing deforestation-causing firewood.
PACKAGING

Pressed Fiber

Biodegradable packaging replacing toxic Styrofoam.
FOOD TECH

Mushroom Substrate

Growing high-yield edible fungi on sterilized cassava waste blocks.
